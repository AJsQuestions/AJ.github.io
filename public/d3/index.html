  • Collection of LaTeX commands to write math

Objective

The commands are designed to make it easy to write math in LaTeX. Of course, many LaTeX editors already provide functionalities to help write mathematical expressions. An additional advantage of these commands, however, is that they are designed so the formulas in LaTeX look as much as possible like the formulas on paper. With these commands, it is therefore easier to read mathematical expressions or write proofs directly in LaTeX.

Features

The commands are tailored to write math for economics and other social sciences—although they might also hopefully be helpful to write math in other fields. The commands introduce the following functionalities:

  • Easily insert brackets that scale automatically
  • Easily insert accents that scale automatically
  • Simple operators: log, exponential, max, min, trace, variance, covariance, correlation, standard deviation, standard error
  • Statistical operators: expectation, probability
  • Easily write derivatives and elasticities, in text or display
  • Easily type key blackboard letters
  • Easily type uppercase calligraphic letters
  • Easily type Greek letters

Brackets

  • \bp{x} gives $\left( x\right)$, where the parentheses scale automatically
  • \bs{x} gives $\left[x\right]$, where the square brackets scale automatically
  • \bc{x} gives $\left\lbrace x\right\rbrace$, where the curly brackets scale automatically
  • \abs{x} gives $|x|$, where the straight brackets scale automatically
  • \norm{x} gives $\left\lVert x \right\rVert$, where the norm signs scale automatically
  • \floor{x} gives $\left\lfloor x\right\rfloor$, where the floor signs scale automatically
  • f\of{x} gives $f(x)$, where the parentheses scale automatically, and where spacing between the function f and the argument (x) is appropriate. f\bp{x} produces $f\left( x\right)$, which introduces too much space between the function f and the argument (x).

Accents

  • \ol{x} gives $\overline{x}$, where the overline scales automatically
  • \ul{x} gives $\underline{x}$, where the underline scales automatically
  • \wh{x} gives $\widehat{x}$, where the hat scales automatically
  • \wt{x} gives $\widetilde{x}$, where the tilde scales automatically

Simple Operators

  • \ln produces $\ln$
  • \ln{x} produces $\ln(x)$

% Exponential operator:

  • \exp produces $\exp$
  • \exp{x} produces $\exp(x)$

% Max operator:

  • \max produces $\max$
  • \max[x] produces $\max_x$
  • \max{y} produces $\max{y}$
  • \max[x]{y} produces $\max_x{y}$

% Min operator:

  • \min produces $\min$
  • \min[x] produces $\min_x$
  • \min{y} produces $\min{y}$
  • \min[x]{y} produces $\min_x{y}$

% Indicator operator:

  • \ind produces $\mathbb{1}$
  • \ind{Y} produces $\mathbb{1}(Y)$

% Trace operator:

  • \tr produces $\operatorname{tr}$
  • \tr{Y} produces $\operatorname{tr}(Y)$

% Variance operator:

  • \var produces $\operatorname{var}$
  • \var{Y} produces $\operatorname{var}(Y)$

% Covariance operator:

  • \cov produces $\operatorname{cov}$
  • \cov{Y} produces $\operatorname{cov}$

% Correlation operator:

  • \corr produces $\operatorname{corr}$
  • \corr{Y} produces $\operatorname{corr}(Y)$

% Standard deviation operator:

  • \sd produces $\operatorname{sd}$
  • \sd{Y} produces $\operatorname{sd}(Y)$

% Standard error operator:

  • \se produces $\operatorname{se}$

  • \se{Y} produces $\operatorname{se}(Y)$

  • \argmax produces $\operatorname{argmax}$

  • \argmin produces $\operatorname{argmin}$

  • \ees produces $\operatorname{ees}$

  • \Re produces $\operatorname{Re}$

  • \Im produces $\operatorname{Im}$

  • \iid gives $\mathbin{\overset{iid}{\sim}}$

  • \as gives $\mathbin{\overset{as}{\to}}$

Statistical Operators

% Expectation operator:

  • \E produces $\mathbb{E}$
  • \E[X] produces $\mathbb{E}_X$
  • \E{Y} produces $\mathbb{E}[Y]$
  • \E[X]{Y} produces $\mathbb{E}_X[Y]$

% Probability operator:

  • \P produces $\mathbb{P}$
  • \P[X] produces $\mathbb{P}_X$
  • \P{Y} produces $\mathbb{P}[Y]$
  • \P[X]{Y} produces $\mathbb{P}_X[Y]$

Derivatives

  • \pd{x}{y} gives the partial derivative of $x$ with respect to $y$: $\frac{\partial x}{\partial y}$
  • \pdx{x}{y} gives the same partial derivative in text mode: $\partial x/\partial y$
  • \pdl{x}{y} gives the partial elasticity of $x$ with respect to $y$: $\frac{\partial\ln x}{\partial\ln y}$
  • \pdlx{x}{y} gives the same partial elasticity in text mode: $\partial\ln(x)/\partial\ln(y)$
  • \od{x}{y} gives the ordinary derivative of $x$ with respect to $y$: $\frac{d x}{d y}$
  • \odx{x}{y} gives the same ordinary derivative in text mode: $d x/d y$
  • \odl{x}{y} gives the ordinary elasticity of $x$ with respect to $y$: $\frac{d\ln x}{d\ln y}$
  • \odlx{x}{y} gives the same ordinary elasticity in text mode: $d\ln(x)/d\ln(y)$
  • \pdw{x}{y}{z} gives the partial elasticity of $x$ with respect to $y$, keeping $z$ fixed: $$\left.\frac{\partial x}{\partial y}\right\vert_{z}$$
  • \pdwx{x}{y}{z} gives the same partial derivative in text mode: $\left.\partial x/\partial y\right\vert_{z}$
